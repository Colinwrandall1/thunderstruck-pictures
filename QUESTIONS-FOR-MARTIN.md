## 2. What to pitch Martin in the call

### 2a. Lock the focus — rental house *or* production company?

Highest-leverage question. The site can do both but it can only **lead with one**. The questionnaire contradicts the original brief: he picked rental-house references, never mentioned Chopper, and the four "first things to see" are all rental gear. The original brief framed him as director-first with rentals as the funding mechanism.

Frame for the conversation: *"Where's the revenue coming from in the next 12 months — gear rentals or production work? Whichever it is, that's what the homepage leads with."*

- **Rental house first** *(our recommendation — fits where his energy and capital actually are)*: Homepage hero is truck/gear. Inventory is the spine. Chopper moves to a secondary card on the homepage and a /productions page. Bio lives on About.
- **Production company first**: Chopper stays as the hero. Rentals becomes a service sub-page. Site reads like a director's portfolio that also rents gear.

### 2b. The phased-launch pitch — "v1 simple, v2 ambitious"

June 22 + the new scope (quote builder + per-product pages + hero videos still to shoot + education) doesn't fit in 3.5 weeks. Better to ship tight on June 22 and grow it than miss the date or ship something half-built.

**Proposed v1 by June 22:**
- Homepage: strong static hero (or one good loop *if* the shoot delivers), brand statement, two clear paths
- Rentals: category-organized inventory with clean per-category photography, quote builder, pricing visible
- About: Martin + Taylor bios
- Productions: single Chopper card with link to choppermovie.com
- Contact: form + socials
- **No** education content, **no** per-product detail pages, **no** background video hero unless the shoot is in the can by ~June 8

**Proposed v2 (separate scope, later):**
- Per-product detail pages with custom photography for hero rental items
- Background video on hero once footage exists and is graded
- Education arm — webinars, courses, membership funnel
- Blog / content marketing layer

The pitch frame: *"Let's launch you on time with a site that looks great, then iterate. You'll have a real URL to send people in 3 weeks instead of 3 months."*

### 2c. The asset-quality reality check

This site is only as good as its photos and video. Big weak hero video hurts the brand more than no hero video. Same for product photography — 350 line items shot poorly is worse than 6 strong category shots.

Frame for the conversation: *"We can design around what you have, not what you hope to have. If the truck shoot lands, we use it. If it doesn't, we ship strong stills and add video in v2. Either way you launch on time."*

Concrete asks tied to this:
- The four videos you listed — actual shoot date? Who's editing, what's the turnaround? **Hard deadline: in-hand by ~June 8 or we ship without them.**
- For the rentals catalog — do you have product photography, or are we using stock manufacturer images for items we don't have shots of? Most rental houses use a mix; want to set expectations.

## Internal notes (Colin, not for the client call)

- **Taylor sign-off is a real blocker.** Before pitching the site externally as "Taylor and Martin's company," have the actual conversation with Taylor. Does he know? Is he OK with FtW-owned gear being represented as Thunderstruck inventory? What's the split if a job books through this site for gear Taylor owns? Liability if Taylor's gear gets damaged on a Thunderstruck rental?
- **Operational risk on the gear list** — if a renter shows up expecting an Arri 35 listed on the site and Taylor's got it in LA, that's a credibility hit. Worth privately telling Martin that listed gear needs to be actually available, or we mark sub-rental items clearly.
- **Quote system build estimate** — static catalog + localStorage cart + Netlify Function to email = ~2–3 days on top of the marketing site, assuming inventory data is well-structured upfront. Worth fronting the data-modeling effort because v2 (per-product pages, etc.) reuses it.
- **Get v1/v2 scope in writing** in whatever proposal/SOW you send. Otherwise Martin will assume per-product pages, video hero, and education are all "phase 1, you're already on the hook."
- **Pricing for v2** — start thinking about whether v2 is a fixed-scope follow-up or a retainer (recurring inventory updates, content additions, hosting, support). Rentals businesses change inventory all the time — a small monthly maintenance retainer fits this client well.
