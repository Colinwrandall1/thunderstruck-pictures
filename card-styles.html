Three directions for the gear cards.

Three ways to present each piece of gear in the rental catalog, shown with real product cutouts. The same four items appear in every row — RED Komodo, Sachtler, Teradek, Nanlux — so you can compare directly. Hover a card to see the motion. Our recommendation is Option 01.

01SpotlitRecommended

Cutout product floating on a dark card over a soft radial spotlight, with a drop shadow grounding it. The B&H / Aputure-premium look — the most cohesive, and it leans into the dark Voltage palette.

Best: premium, cohesive, on-brand  |  Needs: clean cutouts (we have them)

02Light plate

Card stays dark; the product sits in a small light "plate" with the details on the dark area below. Shrinks the bright footprint — the white reads as an intentional gear swatch. Most forgiving of imperfect cutouts.

Best: safest, works with any source image  |  Cost: smaller product, brightest of the set

03Stormfront

Knockouts floating over slowly rolling storm clouds, in translucent cards with viewfinder corner-brackets and a soft reflection. The cloud drift is live — give it a few seconds. The brand baked into the card itself.

Best: unmistakably Thunderstruck, cinematic  |  Best for: a "Featured" row rather than a 50-item catalog
